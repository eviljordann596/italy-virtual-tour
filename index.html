Cultural Experiences in Italy

1. Italian Language

While visiting Florence, tourists often greet each other with “Buongiorno!” (Good morning) or “Ciao!” (Hi/Bye). Learning simple phrases like “Grazie” (Thank you) or “Quanto costa?” (How much does it cost?) helps connect with locals.

2. Art & Architecture

At the Uffizi Gallery, students can virtually admire Botticelli’s “The Birth of Venus”, a masterpiece of the Italian Renaissance. In Rome, learners can explore Pantheon’s perfect dome and understand ancient Roman engineering.

3. Italian Festivals

During the Venice Carnival, people wear colorful masks and costumes. Students can watch videos or participate in virtual mask-making workshops. Another example is the Palio di Siena, a traditional horse race that brings medieval excitement to modern-day Tuscany.
